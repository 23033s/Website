What is Plastic Pollution?


Plastic pollution is when plastic waste ends up in the environment like in our oceans, rivers, streets, and land instead of being properly recycled or thrown away.

Why Plastic Pollution Matters

Because plastic takes hundreds of years to break down, it stays in the environment for a long time and can harm animals, sea life, and even people.
But not just New Zealand is facing plastic pollution.
Plastic Pollution is a massive problem not just in Auckland or New Zealand, but also the whole world.

Kaitiakitanga Quote: "Ko au te whenua, te whenua ko au." - A Māori proverb that translates to "I am the land, the land is me".


This quote signifies a deep, inseparable connection (physical and spiritual) between the Māori people and the land.


The quote is more than just words; it represents a worldview where the land is not simply a resource, but also an essential part of identity, heritage, and wellbeing.

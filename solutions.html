How Do We Stop Plastic Pollution?


Statistics have shown that millions of tonnes of plastic waste enter our oceans every year, harming marine life and polluting ecosystems. While only about 0.5% of plastic waste ends up in the sea, much of the rest is mismanaged on land, leading to widespread environmental damage. These alarming numbers highlight the urgent need for global action to reduce plastic production and improve waste management.


We NEED to take action to put an end to plastic pollution.

Since plastic pollution is one of the biggest environmental challenges today. To stop it, we need to take action on many levels.

Here are some ways to stop plastic pollution:

  • Reduce: Use less single-use plastic like bags, bottles, and packaging.
  • Reuse: Choose reusable alternatives such as cloth bags, refillable bottles, and containers.
  • Recycle: Properly sort and recycle plastics to prevent them from reaching the ocean.
  • Support Policies: Encourage governments to ban harmful plastics and invest in sustainable alternatives.
  • Community Action: Join or organize clean-up events and raise awareness in your community and school.
  • Kaitiakitanga: Honour the Māori value of guardianship of the land by protecting the environment for future generations.

Every small step helps and any sort of help, even if it's small, can make a difference. Together, we can reduce plastic pollution and protect our oceans and wildlife.

Kaitiakitanga Quote: "He taonga te whenua, he koha nā ngā tūpuna." - This whakataukī translates to The land is a treasure, a gift from the ancestors."


This quote states that our environment is a sacred gift, and it is our duty to protect it.


See the land as yourself and protect it, protect it like you would protect yourself.
